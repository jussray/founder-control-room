# Bill Gates — Platform Leverage and Standardization Artifact

## Bottleneck

Economic-development resources exist across agencies, but discovery, eligibility routing, evidence quality, ownership, and outcome tracking are fragmented. The bottleneck is coordination and decision infrastructure, not simply the number of programs.

## Reusable platform assets

- jurisdiction registry and hierarchy;
- source provenance standard;
- program and authority normalization;
- versioned opportunity-scoring contract;
- referral and eligibility workflow primitives;
- outcome metric ledger;
- source adapters and jurisdiction configuration packs;
- proof-gated deployment and rollback records.

## Compounding leverage

Each onboarded jurisdiction should improve adapter coverage, program taxonomies, evidence-quality rules, workflow templates, and outcome benchmarks without sharing private operational records. The reusable knowledge is the schema, adapters, validation logic, and measurement method.

## Standardize centrally

- identifiers and provenance fields;
- score inputs, weights, versions, and bands;
- lifecycle states;
- audit and approval gates;
- privacy classification;
- export and reporting contracts.

## Configure locally

- jurisdiction hierarchy;
- program ownership and eligibility;
- policy constraints;
- source priorities;
- scoring thresholds;
- public visibility;
- outcome targets.

## Scale controls

Do not add a jurisdiction until its accountable owner, authoritative sources, refresh cadence, legal-review boundary, and success metrics are recorded. Growth without these controls compounds bad data rather than platform value.

## Economic model

Near-term value comes from paid implementation, workflow setup, and measurement. Long-term defensibility comes from standardized infrastructure, validated adapters, trust, and cross-jurisdiction operational learning—not ownership of public facts.
