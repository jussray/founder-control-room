# OODA — City-Agnostic Execution Loop

## Observe

Collect official documents, datasets, program pages, procurement signals, market signals, and outcome evidence. Store source time, publisher, confidence, and jurisdiction scope.

## Orient

Normalize the evidence into organization, program, opportunity, and outcome primitives. Identify missing provenance, stale evidence, conflicting authority, and jurisdiction-specific constraints.

## Decide

Score only after the minimum evidence contract is met. Select one action: research further, route a program, prepare a pilot, measure an outcome, revise, close, or reject. The model proposes; the founder and local authority approve.

## Act

Execute the smallest reversible step. Preserve exact source and score versions. Record who authorized the action and what proof is expected next.

## Loop triggers

- a source changes;
- a program opens or closes;
- a new opportunity signal appears;
- a score crosses a configured threshold;
- a pilot produces an outcome;
- evidence becomes stale;
- local authority corrects eligibility or scope.
