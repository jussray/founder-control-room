# ULTRATHINK — City-Agnostic Synthesis

## Strategic conclusion

The defensible product is not a Johnstown dashboard. It is a jurisdiction intelligence layer with Johnstown as the first reference implementation.

## System-level implications

1. **Data architecture:** jurisdiction scope must exist on every source, program, opportunity, and outcome path.
2. **Decision quality:** scores must preserve evidence strength and version history; they cannot erase uncertainty.
3. **Product architecture:** screens consume jurisdiction configuration rather than import a city constant.
4. **Business model:** implementation revenue may be local, but platform leverage comes from reusable adapters, templates, scoring contracts, and outcome reporting.
5. **Trust:** local authorities remain authoritative for law, eligibility, and public communication.
6. **Privacy:** economic operations data must not cross into the Se’kret Bip consumer trust boundary.
7. **Operations:** a new jurisdiction is not ready until source coverage, ownership, freshness, and local review are measured.

## Second-order effects

- More jurisdictions create comparative learning but also increase the risk of misleading rankings.
- More source adapters improve coverage but increase maintenance and provenance burden.
- Public visibility can improve accountability but can expose incomplete hypotheses or sensitive negotiations.
- Automated routing can reduce friction but can amplify bad eligibility rules at scale.

## Decision

Build the reusable core now, keep the first production pilot narrow, and require a real second-jurisdiction onboarding before marketing the platform as fully proven multi-city infrastructure.
