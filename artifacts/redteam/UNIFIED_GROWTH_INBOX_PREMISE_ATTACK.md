# Red Team I — Unified Growth Inbox Premise Attack

## Claim under attack

Combining every business communication channel and adding AI automation will
automatically generate sales, leads, and business growth.

## Failure modes

1. More channels multiply operational mistakes, policy drift, token risk, duplicate
   contacts, and inconsistent brand behavior.
2. Automation can increase message volume while decreasing trust and qualified intent.
3. A unified contact graph can silently merge different people or reuse consent across
   unrelated projects.
4. "AI qualification" can become sensitive-trait targeting or fabricated buying intent.
5. Platform access can be revoked if unofficial automation, message-window violations,
   excessive complaints, or poor quality occur.
6. SMS and voice can create registration, consent, quiet-hours, do-not-call, recording,
   and artificial-voice exposure.
7. Teen-facing Se’kret Bip data could contaminate commercial targeting unless it is
   architecturally excluded.
8. Vanity metrics can mislabel waitlists, booked calls, sponsor pledges, or invoices as
   cash flow.
9. A dead or deprecated provider can absorb build effort without producing reach.
10. "Autopilot" can erase founder authority if dispatch, pricing, discounts, or custom
    terms execute without a distinct approval.

## Conditions under which the system should exist

Build only when the shared layer improves:

- permission and suppression accuracy;
- project separation;
- response speed and quality;
- human handoff;
- attribution;
- auditability;
- collected-revenue measurement;
- provider replacement and rollback.

Do not build a channel merely because an API or MCP exists.

## Premise decision

Proceed with one provider-independent inbox, but begin with first-party, user-initiated
webchat and draft-only assistance. Add external channels only when a real audience,
approved business account, permission model, legal/policy gate, and measurable use case
exist.

There is no unrestricted autonomous cold-outreach mode.
