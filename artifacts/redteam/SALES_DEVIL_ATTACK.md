# Sales + Devil Red Team

## Premise attack

A sales system can become a pressure engine that mistakes activity for value. The default assumption must be that no offer, audience, urgency, authority, or willingness to pay is proven until current evidence establishes it.

Primary failure modes:

- invented or stale demand;
- unclear buyer and decision authority;
- model-generated claims presented as customer truth;
- vanity metrics replacing revenue quality and retention;
- discounts hiding weak positioning or broken unit economics;
- private or sensitive data used for persuasion;
- cross-project catalog, customer, or credential contamination;
- automated outreach or spending without a separate approval.

## Plan attack

The paired `/sales` and `/devil` design fails if agents can skip qualification, omit disqualifiers, recycle stale evidence, or treat a written plan as authorization. It also fails if objections are answered with copy instead of proof.

## Required controls

- 5W1H before offer design;
- explicit fact/inference/unknown separation;
- buyer, authority, fit, and disqualifier record;
- evidence freshness and provenance;
- truthful claims and explicit limitations;
- separate gates for outreach, pricing, discounts, spend, publication, and execution;
- retention, complaint, refund, and margin-quality measurements;
- `/devil` verdict before material execution;
- rollback and kill criteria.

## Verdict

Proceed only as a decision-support contract. Do not grant autonomous sales authority.
