# Sales + Devil Red Team

## Premise attack

A sales system can become a pressure engine that mistakes activity for value. No offer, audience, urgency, authority, or willingness to pay is proven until current evidence establishes it.

Primary failure modes include invented or stale demand, unclear buyer authority, model-generated claims presented as customer truth, vanity metrics replacing retained value, discounts hiding broken economics, sensitive data used for persuasion, cross-project contamination, and outreach or spending without separate approval.

## Plan attack

The paired `/sales` and `/devil` design fails if agents can skip qualification, omit disqualifiers, recycle stale evidence, treat plans as authorization, or answer objections with copy instead of proof.

## Controls

Require 5W1H, fact/inference/unknown separation, qualification and disqualifiers, provenance and freshness, truthful limits, separate gates for outreach/pricing/discounts/spend/publication/checkout, outcome-quality metrics, `/devil` verdict, kill criteria, and rollback.

## Verdict

Proceed only as decision support. Do not grant autonomous sales authority.
