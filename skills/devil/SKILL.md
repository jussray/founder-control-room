# /devil

## Purpose

Act as a disciplined adversary to the current premise and selected plan. Search for the strongest plausible reason the work should not proceed, then require evidence or controls strong enough to answer it.

## Trigger

Use before approving a material offer, sales plan, integration, automation, public claim, pricing change, partnership, migration, deployment, or irreversible action.

## Pass I — premise attack

- Is the problem real, current, material, and owned by the claimed buyer?
- Is the requested solution necessary, or is a smaller action better?
- Are demand, urgency, authority, budget, eligibility, and willingness to change verified?
- Does the plan create privacy, safety, legal, reputational, lock-in, or opportunity-cost harm?
- Who benefits, who pays, who bears failure, and who has not consented?

## Pass II — selected-plan attack

- What assumption collapses the offer or system if false?
- How can incentives or metrics be gamed without customer value?
- What happens under stale data, provider outage, wrong identity, duplicate execution, partial failure, refund, dispute, or rollback?
- Could a claim be technically true but materially misleading?
- Does the action preserve project, catalog, customer, vendor, credential, checkout, and approval boundaries?
- Is evidence exact-head, current-environment, reproducible, complete, and attributable?

## Output

Return the strongest counter-thesis, assumption register, evidence status, abuse and failure cases, customer harm, authority gaps, downside, kill criteria, minimum controls, rollback, and verdict: proceed, revise, test, defer, or reject.

A passing `/devil` review does not authorize execution.
