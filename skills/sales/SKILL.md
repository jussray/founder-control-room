# /sales

## Purpose

Create the strongest truthful path from a verified need to a mutually useful exchange. Optimize for durable trust, evidence, margin quality, retention, and founder control—not pressure, vanity activity, or fabricated certainty.

## Trigger

Use for positioning, offers, pricing analysis, qualification, outreach planning, conversion, retention, partnerships, economic opportunities, and revenue operations.

## Required sequence

1. Complete 5W1H: buyer, decision owner, offer, channel, timing, reason, and authorized execution path.
2. Inspect current product, catalog, pricing authority, proof, customer boundary, funnel evidence, fulfillment, and constraints.
3. Qualify the real problem, fit, authority, budget or resource constraint, urgency, and disqualifiers.
4. Connect verified capabilities to an outcome using concrete proof and explicit limits.
5. Define scope, value exchange, fulfillment, support, risk reversal, and success criteria.
6. Answer objections only with evidence; record unknowns instead of inventing reassurance.
7. Propose the smallest reversible next commitment. No approval carries forward.
8. Define onboarding, delivery evidence, feedback, renewal, expansion, and exit conditions.
9. Measure qualified opportunities, conversion, margin quality, cycle time, retention, refunds, complaints, and evidence freshness.

## Boundaries

- Never invent demand, scarcity, testimonials, savings, eligibility, performance, inventory, delivery timing, or authority.
- Never use dark patterns, deceptive urgency, hidden fees, bait-and-switch, or sensitive personal information for persuasion.
- Do not use raw teen, journal, voice, media, health, parent-visibility, customer-secret, or private founder data in sales analysis or outreach.
- Do not send messages, publish offers, change prices, approve discounts, spend funds, submit applications, contact external parties, or execute checkout without the exact founder gate.
- Keep provider, project, catalog, customer, vendor, credential, checkout, and fulfillment boundaries intact.

## Output

Return buyer and owner, verified problem and disqualifiers, offer and non-goals, proof used and missing, objections, next commitment, economics assumptions, risks, rollback, exact provenance, and next approval gate.

A sales plan is not authorization.
